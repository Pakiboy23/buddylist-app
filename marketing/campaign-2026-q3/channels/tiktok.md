## 0. One divergence from the strategy of record — flagged up front

`campaign-strategy.md` §5.1 sets TikTok cadence at 4–6 posts/week and a KPI of ≥30 posts shipped. **This plan commits to 2–3 posts/week (12–18 posts over the flight).** Rationale:

- Strategy §9 budgets the founder's *total* weekly content load across all channels at ~8–12 hours. Six TikToks/week plus IG, X, Reddit, community ops, and the scorecard does not fit inside that number for one person; the §5.1 figure came from the generic trend-research §d benchmark, not from founder capacity.
- The strategy's own kill-criteria philosophy (§6, §9) prefers a cadence that holds for 6 straight weeks over one that collapses in week 3. Consistency is the differentiator (trend research §d); a broken streak is worse than a modest one.
- The batching plan below can *yield* extra cuts in a good week — 4+/wk is welcome upside, not the commitment.

**Requires campaign-lead sign-off** at or before the Aug 17 phase gate. The posts-shipped KPI in §8 is re-anchored to the honest cadence. Nothing else in §5.1 (role, format, search posture, consent guardrail, volatility clause) changes.

## 1. Binding production rules (apply to every video)

1. **Real UI only.** Every product shot is a screen recording of the shipped iPhone app or web app — RetroWindow glass titlebar chrome, buddy list rows, away-message composer, rooms list, real room conversation. Never mock up UI, never fabricate activity, never show member counts or "N online" (none exist to show — brand brief §2 rule 2, claims register DNC #10).
2. **Member-consent guardrail (strategy §5.1, verbatim intent).** Any capture showing another member's screenname or messages requires that member's explicit, logged consent — practical path: schedule captures with consenting S0 seed members. Otherwise crop or blur ALL non-consenting screennames and message content. **Never publish DM conversation content.** The Buzz demo (Concept 3) is staged in a DM with one consenting seed member and framed so only the buzz event rows and shake animation are visible — no message bodies, consent logged before recording.
3. **Never on camera:** password/credential entry (cut or frame out the sign-in fields), anyone's face or legal name unless it's the founder by his own choice, location-revealing content, anything pressuring a member to reveal identity (brand brief §2 rule 6).
4. **Vocabulary:** no AOL/AIM/ICQ marks or trade dress anywhere in video, on-screen text, caption, or audio (brand brief §7). No dating vocabulary (match, swipe-as-offer, singles, nearby, flirt). The word "lonely" never appears in user-facing content (strategy §1.1 two-track rule). "Hookup" only as the negation "not a hookup app."
5. **Look:** portrait 9:16 (the app is iPhone-only portrait — a native fit). Hero look is dark mode: midnight indigo + amber, boot splash, monospace wordmark, amber pip (brand brief §3). Light cream mode is the approved secondary for daytime-feel posts.
6. **Sound:** no concept depends on a specific licensed track (see §6). Never recreate another company's sign-on/door/buddy sound effects (brand brief §7.5).
7. **Every end card:** "H.I.M." wordmark styled per brand brief §4, `hiitsme.app`, and "18+" (claims #21, #23). CTA points into a **specific room**, not a generic download (strategy §7.1).
8. **Taglines:** Tier 1–3 approved lines only (brand brief §6). "The light's on." does not ship unless founder sign-off lands (strategy §1.1); every script below already works without it.

**Batching plan (this is what makes the cadence hold):** two recording sessions per fortnight, ~90 minutes each — one screen-recording session (batch all UI captures for the coming two weeks, including scheduled captures with consenting seed members) and one voiceover/talking-head session. Week 5 shifts deliberately: Late Night cuts post in the 11pm–1am window, Sunday Reset cuts post Sunday ~6–8pm ET, mirroring the in-app rituals (strategy §7.2). ## 5. Search posture (applies to every post)

TikTok is this audience's search engine (~65% of Gen Z search there — trend research §d). Ranking inputs, in order of weight: caption keywords > spoken words > on-screen text > hashtags.

- **Target query phrases, rotated across captions/VO/on-screen text:** "gay friendship app" · "how to make gay friends" · "how to make gay friends in nyc / chicago / atlanta / la" · "apps that aren't dating apps" · "not a dating app" · "making friends as an adult" · "new in town."
- Say the query phrase **out loud** in at least one beat of every hero cut (VO lines above already do this).
- Captions can run long (2,000+ chars allowed); front-load the query phrase in the first sentence, keep the rest in voice (warm, funny, specific — brand brief §2).
- **Search-presence check:** once a week, search "gay friendship app" and "gay friends app" logged-out and screenshot results into the weekly scorecard (KPI in §8).

## 6. Trend-adaptation guidance

**Format types we adapt (no dependence on any specific licensed sound):**

| Format type | How H.I.M. runs it | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| POV / text-overlay narrative | "POV: it's 1am and one room is awake" over a real Late Night screen recording | Never a POV that implies the viewer is being watched/located |
| Tour / "everything in my ___" | C4 room tour; "every setting a private person needs" (C6 recut) | Real UI only; all 7 rooms shown honestly, cold or warm |
| Prompt / fill-in-the-blank | C3 away-message prompts, re-armed weekly | Replies stay in-thread; repost only per DNC #11 amendment |
| Ranking / tier-list | "Ranking away-message energy" using the real composer | Rank statuses and vibes, never people |
| Founder / build-in-public | C2; honest ship-notes ("this week I fixed the thing where…") | Facts on the record only; no user counts, no testimonials |
| Before/after or "expectation vs reality" | "What people think a gay app looks like → a buddy list and an away message" | Mechanism-level contrast only; no competitor names or logos, no punching at users of other apps |
| Time-anchored ("posting this at 1am") | Week 5 late-night posts recorded and posted in the real window | Real timestamps; never fake a time of day |

**Adoption rules:**

1. **Adapt the format, never license-depend on the sound.** Build every edit to work with original audio (VO + real typing/ambient sound). If a trending sound is used, it must come from TikTok's commercial-library options available to the account type at posting time, and the video must survive the sound being swapped or muted. Never use or recreate legacy IM sound effects owned by other companies (brand brief §7.5).
2. **48–72h decision window.** If a trend can't be shot inside one batching session with real UI and approved claims, skip it. There is always another trend; there is no budget for a scramble.
3. **Automatic declines:** trends requiring face reveals of anyone but the willing founder; "show us where you live/are" location formats; couple/thirst formats (nothing horny-coded — brand brief §2 rule 5); duet/stitch chains built on mocking individuals; anything that pressures members to out themselves (strategy non-goal #9).
4. **Judge on 2-week windows, never single posts** (volatility clause, §5.1 — US algorithm retraining makes single-post reads meaningless). Phase gates Aug 17 and Aug 31: kill formats that lost their window, double formats that won (C3 is the pre-registered favorite to double).
