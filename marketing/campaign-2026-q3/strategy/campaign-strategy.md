## 1. The big idea

### 1.1 Internal name: OPERATION PORCH LIGHT

Internal only — never appears in public copy. The image: the amber online pip next to the H.I.M. wordmark (brand brief §3) is a porch light. It stays on after Pride month's corporate floats pack up, after the gay bars close early or close forever, after every other app has handed you a grid and gone quiet. Someone's up. Come in.

**The idea in one paragraph:** August–September 2026 is the exact moment when the big brands have gone dark on this community (post-Pride retreat, trend research §e), the third places are eroding (trend research §c), and tens of thousands of gay men are moving to new cities for jobs and school (relocation season, trend research flight-timing notes). H.I.M. doesn't market *against* that gloom — it markets the warm, specific, slightly unhinged alternative: a screenname, an away message that says "doomscrolling," a room that's awake at 1am, a buddy list you actually built. We show the real app doing real things, because per trend research §a the highest-performing nostalgia content of 2026 is "real thing that still works," not costume — and our product's actual mechanics *are* the trend.

**Two-track message discipline (from trend research §c, binding):**

- **User-facing track:** sell the fun and the specificity. "A room that's awake at 1am." "Your away message for a Sunday you refuse to leave the house." Never diagnose the user; the word "lonely" does not appear in ads, posts, or captions.
- **Press/earned track:** the friendship-recession + queer-third-places story with a solo-gay-founder protagonist. "Lonely" is allowed in pitches to journalists as sociology, never as a user address.

**Candidate campaign line (needs founder sign-off before public use, per brand brief §6):** "The light's on." — built from era-generic vocabulary, pairs with the amber pip visual, and degrades gracefully into approved Tier 1 lines ("Friendship first." / "Your screenname, your status, your people.") if not approved. All other public copy in this campaign uses the approved Tier 1–3 tagline system as-is.

## 5. Channel architecture

Weighting follows trend research §e: TikTok + Reddit + owned channels carry the plan; Meta surfaces are bonus reach; everything funnels to hiitsme.app (UTM-tagged) and then into a **specific room**, not a generic "download."

### 5.1 TikTok — primary discovery engine

- **Role:** reach + search. TikTok is the audience's search engine (~65% of Gen Z search there — trend research §d); it still surfaces zero-follower accounts; and its nostalgia + place-based discovery trends are H.I.M.-shaped.
- **Format:** screen recordings of the real app — signing on, setting an away message ("doomscrolling," "finally cleaning my apartment"), a buddy signing on, a Buzz shaking the window, a room scrolling at 1am. Zero production budget, natively on-trend, and satisfies brand brief §3 ("lean on real UI"; never mock up fake activity).
- **Member-consent guardrail (binding, all brand-published captures on any channel):** brand-published screen recordings and screenshots may show other members' screennames or messages **only with those members' explicit consent** (practical path: use consenting S0 seed members for room and buddy-list captures, consent logged). Otherwise crop or blur ALL non-consenting screennames and message content before publishing. **Never publish DM content.** Mirrors growth-plan Loop 1 verbatim: "other people's screennames are pseudonymous but not ours to broadcast"; "the norm we model is cropping others' screennames." Rationale: brand brief §2 rule 6 (never outing-adjacent) extends to not exposing a member's pseudonymous presence in a gay app without consent.
- **Search optimization:** captions/on-screen text/spoken words carry query phrases — "gay friendship app," "how to make gay friends in nyc/chicago/atlanta/la," "apps that aren't dating apps," "not a dating app." 3–5 focused hashtags max; keywords in captions outrank hashtags.
- **Cadence:** 4–6 posts/week for all 6 weeks (trend research §d: consistency is the 2026 differentiator; expect no traction before ~8 weeks — this flight builds the base).
- **Volatility clause:** TikTok US algorithm is being retrained post-JV (trend research §e); judge tactics on 2-week windows, never single posts.
- **KPI (secondary to §3 objectives):** ≥30 posts shipped; ≥3 posts over 2,500 organic views; presence in "gay friendship app" search results by flight end.

### 5.2 Instagram — the send-this-to-the-group-chat engine

- **Role:** DM-forwarding velocity, not follower growth. Sends-per-reach is the ranking currency (trend research §d) and the seed community is the initial forwarding network.
- **Format:** static/carousel assets designed to be screenshotted and sent — away-message meme formats ("your away message for a Sunday you refuse to leave the house"), "which room are you" formats, UI jokes in the midnight-indigo-and-amber system. Reels repurposed from TikTok cuts.
- **Cadence:** 3–4 feed posts/week + stories; open a broadcast channel only if following materializes (not a week-1 task).
- **Policy posture:** Meta's GLAAD-documented policy rollbacks (trend research §e) mean reach is treated as a bonus; no paid Meta spend (see §8, §10).
- **KPI:** sends + saves per post (visible in insights), not likes or followers.

### 5.3 Reddit — the disclosed-founder credibility channel

- **Role:** high-intent conversion + the long AI-retrieval tail (honest threads get cited by assistants for years — trend research §d).
- **Playbook:** 90/10 rule, executed as continuation of GTM Tier 3 seeding. Weeks 1–2: founder account participates genuinely in gay men's subreddits (r/askgaybros, r/gaybros — already named in GTM §5), city subreddits, friendship threads — zero product mentions. Week 3: one disclosed founder story post ("I built a friendship app for gay men because I was tired of the grid" register), one-sentence affiliation disclosure, answers every comment. Weeks 4–6: keep participating; product mentioned only where it genuinely answers the question asked.
- **Hard rule:** no stealth marketing, no sockpuppets, ever. One great disclosed post outperforms ten stealth ones, and stealth gets caught (trend research §d).
- **KPI:** one founder post that survives moderation with net-positive reception; referral traffic visible in Vercel referrers and ASC Web Referrer bucket.

### 5.4 X — the build-in-public founder channel

- **Role:** founder narrative + community replies. Not a link channel (non-Premium link posts get near-zero reach — trend research §d).
- **Playbook:** Haaris posts as himself — ship-logs, honest numbers he's comfortable sharing, retro-UI progress clips, the solo-founder texture. Links live in bio and replies only. First 30–60 minutes of reply velocity matter: post when he can actually hang around and reply.
- **Spend:** X Premium (~$8/mo) is authorized — likely the single highest-ROI paid item in the budget (trend research §d).
- **KPI:** replies and profile-click-throughs, not impressions.

Promotional text is the one live ASO lever during the flight.

### 5.6 Press / earned media — support channel

## 7. Feeding the seeded community — rooms must feel alive

The campaign fails if a new user opens a dead room. This section is the bridge between acquisition and the GTM seeding plan, and it obeys the hard law: **presence must be real — never fabricate activity** (brand brief §2 rule 2; `himArtDirection.ts` `liveCount = 0`; DNC #10).

### 7.1 The mechanic

Every campaign asset points **into a specific room**, not at a generic install. A TikTok about Chicago ends on the Chicago room; the Sunday meme ends on Sunday Reset. Acquisition lands where the seed community is already talking — concentrating warmth instead of diluting it across surfaces.

### 7.2 Room programming calendar (standing, all 6 weeks)

| Ritual | Room | When | What |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sunday Reset thread | Sunday Reset | Every Sunday ~6pm ET | Founder or seed member opens the week-planning thread; mirrored as a social post that evening |
| Late Night check-in | Late Night | Nightly, organic | Seed night-owls keep the 11pm–1am window warm; social posts scheduled into the same window in week 5 |
| City check-in prompt | Each regional room | Its Room Tour day (week 3) + weekly thereafter | "Say hi and drop which city/borough you're repping" register (LD §E.3 seeded-welcome voice) |
| Founder presence | All | Daily | Pakiboy24 signed on with a real away message (GTM §5 checklist item, now permanent) |

### 7.3 Seed-community operations

- **First-replier duty:** every new user's first room message gets a human reply within hours. Quiet is framed asynchronously, never apologized for: "People check in throughout the day" (LD §E.7).
- **Ambassador ask (S0):** 10–15 seed members each forward one asset per week to one group chat. In a DM-first distribution era this outperforms anything the brand account posts (trend research §d). The ask is explicit, opt-in, and thanked — not gamified, not tracked invasively.
- **Buddy-loop hospitality:** seed members send buddy requests to new users they've actually talked with (matches the product's accepted-buddy gating, claims #4) — no mass-adding, which would feel like the grid we're against.
- **Aliveness monitoring:** O5 floor checked in the weekly SQL pull (`room_messages` per room per day). If a room goes cold two days running, it gets the next programming slot — the fix is always real conversation, never a fake pulse.
- **What we never do:** no bots, no staff posing as multiple users, no scripted fake conversations, no "N online now" anywhere.

## 10. Non-goals — explicitly out of scope for this flight

1. **Android marketing of any kind.** Nothing public until a Play production rollout is confirmed (DNC #6). Drawer copy gets drafted (§5.5); that is the entire Android motion.
2. **Follower counts as KPIs.** No target for TikTok/IG/X followers anywhere in this plan. The win conditions are §3's product metrics.
3. **Paid influencer placements.** Gifted access and genuine relationships only (§5.8). No contracts, no scripted reads.
4. **Meta ad spend.** $0 this flight (§8).
5. **Building a referral/invite-link mechanic as a campaign dependency.** The share-link plumbing doesn't work and "invite link" copy is banned (DNC #7, baseline audit gap 4). The in-app buddy invite (#17) may be described; no growth loop is built on it this quarter.
6. **Scale claims, social proof, or manufactured FOMO.** No user counts, no testimonials, no "join thousands," no fake urgency (DNC #10, #11). Honest smallness is the brand (brand brief §2 rule 2).
7. **Dating-market positioning.** No dating keywords in ASO, no dating-adjacent creative, no "not like other dating apps" framing that concedes the category. We are a friendship app that is not in that aisle (brand brief §5).
8. **Naming competitors in public copy.** Press conversations follow the GTM §4 mechanism-level register only (brand brief §5).
9. **Any mechanic pressuring identity or location disclosure.** No "post your face" trends, no location tagging asks, no "who's near you" framing — regional rooms are chosen, not detected (brand brief §2 rule 6, claims #22).
10. **Moderation promises.** No SLA, no "team," no "24/7" (DNC #3, #4). "Every report is reviewed." Full stop.
11. **New analytics vendors or native-app tracking.** Measurement stays within ASC + Vercel + first-party Supabase SQL, preserving the Tracking = No label posture (baseline audit §3.3). The only sanctioned addition is the first-party `acquisition_source` column if the founder budgets it.
12. **International expansion, iPad, web-feature parity pushes, or product roadmap commitments in marketing copy.** We sell what shipped (claims register, whole document).
