# H.I.M. Q3 2026 Campaign — Approved Claims Register

**Purpose:** Every public-facing campaign asset (social copy, landing pages, store listing updates, press notes, ads) must be checked against this register before publishing. If a product claim is not in the APPROVED table, it does not ship. If it appears in the DO-NOT-CLAIM table, it never ships in any phrasing.

**Verified:** 2026-07-15, directly against the codebase at repo `buddylist-app` (main branch, HEAD `35f76e2`). Evidence paths are repo-relative. Re-verify before reuse in future campaigns — feature state changes.

**Scope reminder (per campaign brief):** audience is gay men; tone warm, funny, specific, community-first. Never use AOL / AIM / ICQ trademarks in public copy or imply affiliation. No fabricated testimonials, stats, press, or user counts.

## Nuances the Content Creator should keep in back pocket (accurate, but verify tone before use)

- **Push permission respect:** the app never requests notification permission on cold launch — only after a deliberate user action (`MEMORY.md` decision 2026-05-17, `src/lib/nativePush.ts`). Quietly great for a privacy-forward audience.
- **Flagged-message honesty:** the sender always sees their own flagged text; only recipients get the placeholder (`src/lib/contentModeration.ts` `displayBodyForMessage`). Don't describe the filter as "blocking" messages — it hides them from recipients pending review.
- **App-facing copy is de-gendered** ("gay men" was removed from in-app copy per PR #60 for App Review; marketing/community copy still targets gay men per campaign brief). Keep store listing and in-app claims consistent with what shipped.
- **Stale source of truth warning:** `him-launch-deliverables.md` contains two now-wrong details — "full copy of your data" (see DNC #2) and "17+" (see Approved #23). This register supersedes it for claims.
