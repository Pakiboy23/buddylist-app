1. What we collect

Account & identity

When you register, you choose a screenname. We construct a synthetic internal email address ({screenname}@hiitsme.app) used only by our authentication system — it is never shown publicly and has no mailbox. If you add a real email address for password-reset purposes, we store that too. We also store a hashed password (we never store or see your plaintext password), a profile bio and status message that you choose to share, and an optional avatar image you upload.

Messages & media

We store the content of direct messages you send and receive, and messages you post in chat rooms. This includes message body text, timestamps, delivery and read-receipt metadata, reply and forward references, emoji reactions, and edits or deletions. If you send media attachments (images, video, audio, documents up to 10 MB), those files and their metadata (filename, size, MIME type, dimensions) are stored.

Messages you mark as disappearing are assigned an expiry timestamp. Automated deletion of expired messages is a planned feature; until implemented, content may persist past the expiry time.

Social graph

We store your buddy list (who you have added), connection status (following / pending / mutual), and block records including any optional reason you provide (up to 240 characters). Trust & safety abuse reports you file or receive are stored with category, detail text (up to 1,200 characters), and moderation status.

Presence & activity

We collect last-active timestamps, idle timestamps, your current room memberships and last-seen position in each room, and unread-message state. These power the live buddy-list and room-presence features.

Push tokens & device information

When you grant notification permission, we store your APNs device token (iOS), the platform (ios / android / web), and whether the token is for sandbox or production. Stale tokens are automatically removed when Apple reports them as invalid. We do not collect your device model, OS version, advertising ID, or any other device identifier beyond what is needed for notification delivery.

Preferences & settings

We store your privacy preferences: whether to share read receipts, your notification preview mode (full message, name only, or hidden), whether the screen shield (screenshot blocker) is enabled, and per-conversation DM preferences. We also store a discoverability flag controlling whether your account appears in search results.

Authentication logs & IP addresses

Our authentication provider (Supabase) logs the IP address associated with each sign-in and session refresh. Our web hosting and API infrastructure (Vercel) logs IP addresses for inbound requests. These logs are retained per our sub-processors' standard log retention policies and are used solely for fraud detection, abuse prevention, and security investigation.

Web analytics

On the web version of H.I.M., we use Vercel Analytics to collect anonymised page-view events and device-type breakdowns. No cookies are set for analytics purposes.

2. Why we collect it

  • To provide the service — accounts, authentication, message delivery, room presence, buddy list, media sharing, push notifications.
  • To keep the service safe — content moderation (automated keyword filter on messages before they are stored), abuse reporting, blocking, account suspension.
  • To let you recover access — password reset via email if you have added a real address.
  • To understand usage in aggregate — anonymised analytics to inform product decisions.
  • To comply with law — retaining moderation records where required by applicable law.

We do not use your data for advertising, do not sell it to third parties, and do not build advertising profiles.

3. Lawful bases (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we rely on the following lawful bases:

  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): Account registration, message storage and delivery, room membership, social graph, media storage, presence, preferences. Processing is necessary to perform the service you signed up for.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): Security logs, IP-address retention for fraud/abuse detection, aggregate analytics, stale-token pruning, automated content moderation. Our legitimate interests are balanced against your rights; the sensitivity of this app's data weighs heavily in that balance.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): Push notification delivery; message-content previews in notifications (gated by your notification preview setting). You can withdraw consent by disabling notifications in iOS Settings → H.I.M. → Notifications, or by switching your notification preview mode to "hidden" in the app.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): Retention of certain moderation records where required by law.

4. Sensitive & special-category data

Important disclosure. H.I.M. is a messaging app whose community is predominantly LGBTQ+. By creating an account and using the service, your membership in the app's community, your social connections, your messages, and your room memberships may, in combination, be reasonably inferred to reveal information about your sexual orientation or gender identity. Under GDPR Article 9, this constitutes special-category personal data requiring a higher standard of protection.

We process this data only to provide the core features of the app you chose to use. We apply the following additional safeguards:

  • All data is stored with row-level security; other users can only access data they are permitted to see.
  • Message content is encrypted in transit (TLS) and stored in an access-controlled database.
  • We do not cross-reference your H.I.M. data with any external database or advertising network.
  • Account deletion (Account → Delete account in the app) permanently erases your profile, messages, connections, and all other personal data from our systems.
  • Push notification previews are gated by your privacy setting; set it to "hidden" to prevent message content from appearing on your lock screen.

We process special-category data on the basis of your explicit consent given when you create an account and voluntarily share this information with other users, and/or because you have manifestly made this information public through your use of the service (Art. 9(2)(a) and/or Art. 9(2)(e)).

5. Push notifications & lock screen

When you enable push notifications, H.I.M. sends alerts when buddies send you a message. The notification payload includes the sender's screenname and — depending on your privacy setting — a preview of the message (up to 140 characters).

Your notification preview mode controls what appears on your lock screen:

  • Full — sender name and message preview are shown in the banner and on the lock screen.
  • Sender only (default for new accounts) — sender name is shown; message content is hidden.
  • Hidden — the notification is delivered with no sender name or message content visible anywhere on the lock screen.

Lock-screen privacy notice: Notification banners and the lock screen are visible to anyone with physical access to your device. If you are in a situation where others can see your screen, select Sender only or Hidden to prevent message content or contact identities from being exposed. The Hidden mode shows only a generic "New message" alert with no identifying information.

You can change this setting at any time in Account → Notification previews. You can disable all push notifications at any time in iOS Settings → H.I.M. → Notifications. Disabling notifications does not delete your account or any data.

We do not use push notifications for marketing. Notifications are sent only in direct response to messages you receive from other users.

6. On-device storage

H.I.M. stores the following data locally on your device only. This data is never transmitted to our servers except as part of normal app operation (e.g., the outbox sends its content once connectivity is restored).

  • Outbox — messages you compose while offline, queued for delivery. Cleared automatically once delivered.
  • App lock — whether the PIN lock is enabled, a SHA-256 hash of your PIN (never the PIN itself), auto-lock duration, and whether biometric authentication is enabled. Your biometric data (Face ID / Touch ID) is processed entirely by the device's Secure Enclave and is never accessible to H.I.M.
  • Privacy preferences cache — a local copy of your notification preview mode and per-conversation settings for faster rendering.
  • Room state — joined room list and unread counts, cached for up to 7 days.

All local data is removed when you delete the app from your device or delete your account through the in-app flow.

7. Sub-processors & international transfers

We use the following third-party services that process personal data on our behalf. All transfers of EEA/UK personal data to processors in the United States are conducted under appropriate transfer mechanisms (Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent).

  • Supabase, Inc. (United States) — database, authentication, file storage, realtime messaging, serverless functions. All user data, messages, and files are hosted on Supabase infrastructure. Supabase is SOC 2 Type II certified and operates a Data Processing Agreement.
  • Vercel, Inc. (United States) — web hosting, serverless API functions, and web analytics. Request IP addresses and user-agent strings are processed by Vercel infrastructure on the web platform. Vercel is SOC 2 Type II certified and operates a Data Processing Agreement.
  • Apple Inc. — Apple Push Notification service (APNs) — iOS push notification delivery. When you enable notifications, your APNs device token and the notification payload (sender name and, depending on your privacy setting, a message preview) pass through Apple's infrastructure. Apple acts as a data processor for this purpose.

We do not use Google Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) at this time. If we add Android push notifications in future, this policy will be updated.

All other libraries used in the app (Capacitor, jose, bad-words) operate locally on your device and transmit no data to third parties.

8. How long we keep data

  • Account and profile data: Until you delete your account.
  • Messages and media: Until the sender or recipient deletes their account. Disappearing messages are assigned an expiry; automated deletion of expired content is a planned feature.
  • Push tokens: Until invalidated by Apple (automatic) or you delete your account.
  • Abuse reports: Retained after account deletion where required for legal-obligation purposes.
  • Server logs (authentication, API): Per the standard retention policies of Supabase and Vercel for your service tier.
  • Analytics: Per Vercel Analytics' retention policy (anonymised aggregates).
  • On-device data: Until you delete the app or delete your account.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have some or all of the following rights regarding your personal data. To exercise any right, contact us at support@hiitsme.app. We will respond within 30 days (GDPR) or 45 days (CCPA/CPRA).

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Deletion: Delete your account and all associated data directly in the app at Account → Delete account. This is the fastest and most complete deletion path. You may also request deletion by email.
  • Correction: Update your screenname, bio, status message, email, or password directly in the app. Contact us for other corrections.
  • Export / portability: Request a machine-readable export of your personal data.
  • Objection to legitimate-interest processing: You may object to processing based on legitimate interests (e.g., analytics). We will assess whether our interests override your rights in the specific circumstances.
  • Withdraw consent: Withdraw push notification consent at any time in iOS Settings. Withdraw notification-preview consent by switching to "name only" or "hidden" in Account → Notifications.
  • Lodge a complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe we have breached applicable law.

We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of these rights.

10. California rights (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) give you additional rights.

Categories of personal information collected

We collect identifiers (screenname, synthetic email), internet or network activity (IP address, session data), geolocation data (city-level, derived from IP by our infrastructure providers), and the contents of messages and communications. Messages, room memberships, and social-graph data may constitute Sensitive Personal Information under the CPRA because they reveal the contents of communications and, in the context of this app, may reveal information about sexual orientation or gender identity.

Right to opt out of sharing / sale

We do not sell your personal information and do not share it with third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising. You do not need to take any action to opt out.

Right to limit use of sensitive personal information

As a California resident, you have the right to request that we limit our use of sensitive personal information to the purposes permitted by the CPRA (i.e., providing the service you requested). We already limit our use of sensitive personal information in this way. To exercise this right formally, contact support@hiitsme.app.

Other CCPA / CPRA rights

You have the right to know what personal information we collect, delete your personal information, correct inaccurate information, and opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information. To exercise these rights, contact support@hiitsme.app or use the in-app account deletion flow.

If we learn that a user under 17 has created an account, we will delete the account and all associated data. If you believe a minor has created an account, please contact support@hiitsme.app.
