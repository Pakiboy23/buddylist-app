## 5. Seeding Strategy — Pre-Launch Community Plan

### Goal
25–40 active users before any public announcement. Enough that a new user signing up sees: active buddy lists, recent away messages, live chat rooms, and DM activity. The product has to feel alive on day one.

### Who to seed

**Tier 1 — Personal network (target: 10–15)**
People Haaris knows who will actually use it and give feedback. Gay friends, acquaintances, anyone in the community who'll try something new if asked directly.

**Tier 2 — Gay Orlando / Central Florida (target: 10–15)**
Local is easier to seed because you can follow up in person. Gay bars, local queer community spaces, LGBTQ+ Orlando groups on Facebook or Instagram. Direct outreach with "I built this and I need real users before I launch — can you try it?"

**Tier 3 — Gay social media (target: 5–10)**
Gay Twitter/X, gay subreddits (r/askgaybros, r/gaybros), Instagram queer community pages. Not a broad announcement — a specific post: "I built a friendship app for gay men and I need early testers. DM me if you want in."

### Seeding checklist

- [ ] Create 5–8 starter chat rooms with real topic names (not placeholders): "New in [city]", "Desi & Gay", "Night Owls", "Gay men who cook", "What are you watching"
- [ ] Haaris's screenname (Pakiboy24) should be active with a real away message
- [ ] At least 3–5 buddy pairs active before any new user signs up
- [ ] Welcome message or onboarding hint that tells new users: set your away message first, then browse rooms
- [ ] Private waitlist/Discord or group chat for early testers to give feedback and feel ownership

### Timing
Seed phase: 3–4 weeks before any public announcement or press pitch. No public post until the product feels warm.
